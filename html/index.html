Designing a subsystem for a software-defined radio

Our goal is to design an audiophile’s dream radio. This will be made possible through the design of a software-defined radio (SDR). It is similar to the traditional radio that we are all familiar with, except certain parts are implemented using software and digital processes instead of purely electrical components. Its goal is still to achieve radio communication, but the fact that it is software-defined means that a lot of the radio’s functionality can be easily modified at any time [1]. This document outlines the purpose for a demodulator and audio amplifier (DAA / Subsystem C) system in an SDR, which is one of six subsystems in a modular SDR system. It begins by exploring the general applications of SDRs, followed by the operations fulfilled by the DAA and the proposed subsystem design. The document then concludes with descriptions of a series of tests to verify the functionality of various aspects of the design, to ensure that the DAA is of audiophile quality.
